ESG Predictor tool

Overview

The increasing global focus on Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria emphasizes their growing importance in corporate strategies and investment policies. Nevertheless, there is a low level of transparency and data availability because of inconsistencies in methodologies between ESG rating providers and the voluntary nature of disclosed ESG information by the corporates.

This research has a major contribution to ESG predictions by demonstrating the potential for neural network models integrated with financial metrics to provide a new, more reliable way of assessing ESG based on universally available financial data to promise an upcoming global standard and higher reliability of investment decisions based on ESG criteria.

Key findings

Divergences in Methods by ESG Rating Providers

The​re still exists a huge variance in methodologies for ESG ratings among the ESG rating providers. This research suggests that some level of standardization in scoring methodologies be considered, crucial for the informed decision-making of stakeholders.

Discrepancies in Governance scores

The model successfully predicts ESG scores in general, with particularly better predictions for E and S scores compared to G scores. This is due to the limited information on governance structures in structured financial data, while Environmental and Social factors show higher correlations with financial indicators. It identifies areas for improvement in Governance score prediction.

Main Determinants of ESG Ratings

Companies with higher revenues tend to have better ESG scores because disclosing ESG information can enhance their image and reputation, leading to better revenue. In addition to that, the ESG ratings vary significantly across different regions and industries.
